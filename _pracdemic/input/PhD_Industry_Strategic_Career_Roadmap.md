# <a id="_Toc224818326"></a>90\-Day Strategic Action Plan

This action plan is organized into three 30\-day phases, each building on the previous\. The goal: within 90 days, have a full\-time professor of practice application pipeline active, a consulting brand launched, and a thought leadership cadence established\.

## <a id="_Toc224818327"></a>Phase 1: Foundation \(Days 1–30\)

__Theme: __Secure the anchor, activate the brand

### <a id="_Toc224818328"></a>Academic Job Search Launch

1. Create accounts on HigherEdJobs, Chronicle Vitae, AIS Academic Careers, and INFORMS Career Center\. Set alerts for: professor of practice, clinical professor, teaching professor, and lecturer positions in IS, IT Management, Business Analytics, and Digital Transformation\.
2. Prepare a dedicated academic cover letter template emphasizing three differentiators: \(a\) 25\+ years of enterprise IT leadership at Oracle Hospitality, \(b\) PhD in Business Administration completed 2024, and \(c\) demonstrated teaching effectiveness with MBA and MS graduate students at an R1 university\.
3. Request updated teaching evaluations from FAU Executive Education program\. Compile a teaching portfolio with syllabi, sample assignments, and student feedback\.
4. Identify and apply to 5–10 current openings\. Priority targets include Tulane \(Professor of Practice in IT, start June 2026\), UMBC \(Assistant Teaching Professor in IS, Fall 2026\), and Texas State \(Assistant Professor of Practice in ISA\)\.
5. Contact department chairs at FIU \(Information Systems and Business Analytics\), University of Miami, and Nova Southeastern University to inquire about upcoming practice\-oriented faculty openings\.

### <a id="_Toc224818329"></a>LinkedIn and Personal Brand Setup

1. Rewrite LinkedIn headline to: “PhD | Global IT Operations Leader | Adjunct Instructor | Hospitality Technology & Digital Transformation”
2. Craft a new About section organized around three content pillars: \(1\) Enterprise IT operations and digital transformation, \(2\) Hospitality technology strategy and cloud migration, \(3\) Bridging academic research and industry practice\.
3. Publish first two LinkedIn articles: one on lessons from Oracle Hospitality’s OPERA Cloud transformation, and one on what MBA students need to know about IT operations management that textbooks miss\.
4. Connect with 50 targeted professionals: department chairs at target universities, hospitality technology leaders, Big 4 digital transformation partners, and AACSB leadership\.

### <a id="_Toc224818330"></a>Journal Article Progress

1. Complete the POLR \(Provisional Outline Literature Review\) restructured around your single theoretical framework, per Chris’s guidance\.
2. Finalize target journal selection based on your citation frequency analysis\.
3. Submit first draft of restructured article to Chris for review by Day 30\.

## <a id="_Toc224818331"></a>Phase 2: Acceleration \(Days 31–60\)

__Theme: __Build pipeline, establish thought leadership cadence

### <a id="_Toc224818332"></a>Consulting Brand Development

1. Register a consulting LLC in Florida \(name suggestion: align with your expertise in hospitality technology and digital transformation\)\.
2. Create a one\-page service offering document covering three engagement types: \(a\) Fractional CIO/CDO for hospitality companies \($10K–$20K/month\), \(b\) OPERA Cloud migration advisory \($200–$400/hour\), \(c\) Executive education and corporate training workshops \($5K–$15K per session\)\.
3. Register on platforms: Toptal \(fractional executive\), Go Fractional, GLG \(expert network\), and Guidepoint\.
4. Join Cayuga Hospitality Consultants network and HFTP \(Hospitality Financial and Technology Professionals\) as prospective consulting partner\.
5. Reach out to 3–5 Oracle Hospitality partner ecosystem contacts for potential advisory or consulting referrals\.

### <a id="_Toc224818333"></a>Thought Leadership Cadence

- Establish a weekly LinkedIn posting rhythm: 1 long\-form article per month, 2 insight posts per week \(industry commentary, teaching reflections, or research\-to\-practice translations\)\.
- Submit a practitioner article pitch to Cornell Hospitality Quarterly or Hospitality Upgrade on AI adoption in hotel operations\.
- Propose a conference presentation or panel at HITEC \(Hospitality Industry Technology Exposition and Conference\) 2026 or HFTP Annual Convention\.

### <a id="_Toc224818334"></a>Network Activation

- Schedule 10–15 informational conversations with: department chairs at target universities, former Oracle colleagues now in consulting, hospitality CIOs and CTOs at major hotel chains, and Big 4 directors in hospitality technology practices\.
- Join AACSB Bridge to Practice community and AIS \(Association for Information Systems\) as networking investments\.

## <a id="_Toc224818335"></a>Phase 3: Conversion \(Days 61–90\)

__Theme: __Close opportunities, build momentum

1. Follow up on all academic applications\. If any are progressing, prepare teaching demonstrations and research presentations tailored to each institution\.
2. Convert at least one consulting lead into a paid engagement, even if small \(a technology assessment or half\-day executive workshop\)\.
3. Apply to 2–3 advisory board positions at hospitality technology startups or South Florida tech companies\.
4. Finalize and submit the journal article to target journal\.
5. Evaluate month\-3 LinkedIn analytics: target 500\+ new connections, 10K\+ impressions per month, and 2–3 inbound inquiries\.
6. Assess portfolio career mix and adjust weighting based on what’s gaining traction\.

## <a id="_Toc224818338"></a>Why the Academic Anchor Matters

- __AACSB SA Status: __A PhD completed in 2024 provides Scholarly Academic classification for approximately five years \(through ~2029\), making you maximally valuable for accreditation compliance—the most constrained faculty category\.
- __Institutional Platform: __A full\-time faculty appointment provides the institutional affiliation, credibility, and infrastructure needed to attract consulting clients, advisory board invitations, and speaking engagements\.
- __Income Stability: __A 9–10 month contract with benefits provides a reliable base income while summers remain free for consulting, executive education, and research\.
- __Publication Pipeline: __Faculty status provides access to journal networks, co\-authorship opportunities, conference funding, and the research infrastructure needed to maintain SA status\.
- __3–5x Pay Increase: __Transitioning from adjunct \($4,093/course national average\) to full\-time professor of practice \($120K–$175K\) represents a transformative income change for equivalent teaching loads\.

# <a id="_Toc224818343"></a>Consulting & Fractional C\-Suite Positioning

## <a id="_Toc224818344"></a>Your Unique Consulting Niche

The intersection of Oracle Hospitality OPERA Cloud expertise, 25\+ years of global IT operations leadership, and doctoral\-level research credentials creates a defensible consulting niche that virtually no other individual can match\. OPERA Cloud is deployed across 233 countries, and Oracle Hospitality was named a Leader in the IDC MarketScape for Hospitality PMS in 2025\. The global hotel PMS market is approximately $3 billion annually with 7–8% CAGR\.

## <a id="_Toc224818345"></a>Three Engagement Models

__Model A: Fractional CIO/CDO for Hospitality Companies__

- Target: mid\-market hotel groups \(50–500 properties\) undergoing cloud migration or digital transformation
- Engagement: 1–2 days/week, 6–12 month retainer
- Rate: $10K–$20K/month \($120K–$240K annualized per client\)
- Differentiator: PhD\-level strategic rigor \+ deep OPERA platform knowledge \+ proven enterprise IT leadership

__Model B: OPERA Cloud Migration & Technology Advisory__

- Target: individual hotel properties or small chains migrating from legacy PMS to OPERA Cloud
- Engagement: project\-based, 3–6 months
- Rate: $200–$400/hour or fixed\-fee technology assessments \($15K–$50K\)
- Differentiator: insider understanding of OPERA architecture, integration patterns, and organizational change requirements

__Model C: Executive Education & Corporate Workshops__

- Target: hotel management companies, hospitality industry associations \(HFTP, HTNG, AHLA\), and university executive education programs
- Topics: Digital Transformation Strategy, AI in Hospitality Operations, IT Governance for Hotel Groups, Cloud Migration Leadership
- Rate: $5K–$15K per session; $25K–$75K for multi\-day programs
- Differentiator: unique ability to blend academic frameworks with real\-world Oracle Hospitality case studies

# <a id="_Toc224818356"></a>Strategic Summary

The market for industry\-experienced PhDs in business and IT has never been stronger\. Six converging forces—the academic relevance crisis, digital transformation talent shortage, AI leadership explosion, the rise of the pracademic model, remote work normalization, and above\-average BLS growth projections—create unprecedented demand for exactly this profile\.

## <a id="_Toc224818357"></a>Five Highest\-Priority Actions

1. __Transition from adjunct to full\-time professor of practice\. __This is the single most important career move\. Apply to 5–10 current openings immediately while pursuing the internal pathway at FAU\. The academic anchor provides stability, credibility, benefits, and a platform for all other income streams\.
2. __Launch consulting brand and register on expert networks\. __Your Oracle Hospitality OPERA Cloud expertise is a globally scarce asset\. Monetize it through fractional CIO work, technology advisory, and expert network calls while the knowledge is maximally current\.
3. __Establish LinkedIn thought leadership cadence\. __Two posts per week plus one article per month\. Content pillars: hospitality technology, enterprise IT leadership, and bridging research\-practice\. This drives inbound opportunities for both academic and consulting roles\.
4. __Submit the journal article\. __Complete the POLR restructuring, finalize with Chris, and submit to target journal by Q2 2026\. This is essential for maintaining AACSB SA status and demonstrating scholarly productivity to faculty search committees\.
5. __Build the South Florida network\. __Schedule 15\+ informational conversations over 60 days with department chairs \(FIU, UM, NSU\), hospitality CIOs, Big 4 partners, and HFTP/Cayuga contacts\. The Miami\-Orlando hospitality technology corridor is your most defensible geographic market\.

*The combination of Oracle Hospitality global leadership, a 2024 PhD, and R1 adjunct teaching is not just competitive—it fills a structural gap that business schools, consulting firms, and technology\-driven industries are all actively trying to close\.*
